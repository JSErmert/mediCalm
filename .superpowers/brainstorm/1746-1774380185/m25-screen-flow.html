Expression Profiles — Orb Behavior by Session Type

Derived from dominant mechanism + severity. Visual-layer only — timing durations never change.

Nerve / Burning
MECH_NERVE_IRRITATION · any severity
  • Glow amplitude narrow (0.40–0.50)
  • Glow opacity reduced (0.25–0.40)
  • Scale range tight (0.75–0.95)
  • Cue density minimal (3-cue only)
  • Feel quieter, less stimulus
Severe / Acute
pain ≥ 7 · overprotection or compression
  • Glow amplitude soft (0.45–0.60)
  • Glow opacity low (0.20–0.35)
  • Scale range full (0.72–1.0)
  • Cue density minimal (3-cue only)
  • Feel slow, anchoring
Moderate
pain 4–6 · rib restriction or cervical
  • Glow amplitude standard (0.55–0.85)
  • Glow opacity standard (0.35–0.55)
  • Scale range full (0.72–1.0)
  • Cue density standard (3 cues + microtext)
  • Feel balanced, guiding
Gentle / Low
pain ≤ 3 · any mechanism
  • Glow amplitude full (0.55–0.85)
  • Glow opacity higher (0.40–0.60)
  • Scale range full (0.72–1.0)
  • Cue density full (3 cues + microtext)
  • Feel present, open
One High-Impact Question

The M2 spec explicitly excluded a round counter ("adds cognitive load with no benefit"). M2.5 adds it back for progress orientation and trust. How should it be shown?

A

Dot strip (recommended)

A row of 8 small dots, filled as rounds complete. Subtle, peripheral, no reading required. Lives in a fixed position above or below the orb. Similar to audio waveform step indicators.

B

Numeric "3 / 8"

Small muted text showing current round / total. Explicit and readable but requires interpretation. Works well if users feel lost without a number. Positioned tertiary — same zone as protocol name.

C

Progress arc on orb

A thin arc drawn around the orb perimeter that fills as rounds complete. Integrates with the orb as the focal point. More elegant but adds visual complexity to the primary instrument.
