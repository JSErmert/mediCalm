**mediCalm UX/UI Experience Report**  
  
**Overview**  
  
mediCalm is a real-time, medically grounded pain-interruption application designed to guide users through immediate relief using minimal, precise, and calming instruction. The system transforms user-reported pain into a structured, three-step intervention combined with guided breathwork.  
  
The experience is designed for users in pain—where cognitive load must be minimized and guidance must feel intuitive, trustworthy, and immediate.  
  
⸻  
  
**Core Experience Philosophy**  
  
mediCalm is not a wellness app.  
  
It is a **real-time decision engine** that:  
It is a **real-time decision engine** that:  
	•	interprets pain input  
	•	identifies patterns  
	•	delivers immediate, safe interventions  
  
The system must feel:  
	•	clinically grounded  
	•	calm and reassuring  
	•	fast in response  
	•	slow in experience  
  
**Philosophical Foundation**  
  
mediCalm is built on a core principle:  
  
**Respect pain. Respond with deliberate, supportive action.**  
  
The app does not promise outcomes.  
It provides clear, grounded guidance so users can engage with their pain constructively.  
  
The experience should communicate:  
	•	“You’re here. Start here.”  
	•	“Follow this.”  
	•	“Good. Continue.”  
  
This creates:  
	•	trust without overpromising  
	•	support without emotional overreach  
	•	clarity without cognitive overload  
  
mediCalm exists for anyone who wants to:  
	•	ease discomfort  
	•	respond instead of react  
	•	take small, guided steps toward relief

**Primary User Flow**  
  
**1. Immediate Entry**  
  
Upon opening the app, the user is prompted:  
  
**“What level is your pain right now?”**  
**“What level is your pain right now?”**  
  
User inputs:  
	•	Pain level (0–10)  
	•	Pain region(s)  
	•	Pain type(s): burning, tight, sore, inflammation, pressure, sharp, throbbing  
	•	Optional context: standing, sitting, driving, stress, overhead movement, post-sleep

**2. Instant Response**  
  
The app immediately processes the input and generates a personalized intervention.  
  
There is **no delay**.  
There is **no delay**.

**3. Guided 3-Step Sequence**  
  
The intervention is presented as a **three-step sequence**, appearing progressively:  
	•	Step 1: appears immediately  
	•	Step 2: appears after ~3 seconds  
	•	Step 3: appears after ~3 seconds  
  
Each step:  
	•	contains 5–7 essential words  
	•	ends with a period  
	•	avoids unnecessary explanation  
  
Example:  
  
Step 1:  
Lean forward. Support your body.  
  
Step 2:  
Press ball into tight area.  
  
Step 3:  
Inhale ribs. Exhale slowly longer.

**Session Completion & Tracking**  
  
To make mediCalm feel full and lived-in, the experience should not end when the guided sequence ends.  
  
After each completed session, the app should record a lightweight session entry for the user.  
  
**Track:**  
	•	timestamp  
	•	pain before session  
	•	pain after session  
	•	selected region(s)  
	•	selected pain type(s)  
	•	sequence delivered  
	•	session duration  
	•	user response: helped a lot / helped somewhat / no change  
	•	optional note  
  
**Why this matters**  
  
Session tracking makes mediCalm feel:  
	•	personal  
	•	useful over time  
	•	clinically organized  
	•	more complete as a product  
  
It also helps the user see:  
	•	which sequences help most  
	•	what pain patterns recur  
	•	whether their pain tends to improve with certain interventions  
  
**UX Principle**  
  
Tracking should feel quiet and effortless.  
It should never interrupt relief.  
  
Suggested flow:  
	•	sequence completes  
	•	soft completion screen fades in  
	•	user sees pain check-out prompt  
	•	session is saved automatically after confirmation  
  
**Completion Tone**  
  
The completion state should feel:  
	•	calm  
	•	respectful  
	•	encouraging without exaggeration  
  
Examples:  
	•	“Session complete.”  
	•	“Notice what changed.”  
	•	“Save your response.”

**Inspiration Boundary**  
  
mediCalm can absolutely take inspiration from other breathing or recovery apps at the level of:  
	•	flow  
	•	pacing  
	•	sense of immersion  
	•	session completion architecture  
	•	consistency of visual experience  
  
But it should not copy:  
	•	branded visual signatures  
	•	exact layouts  
	•	proprietary flows  
	•	distinctive wording  
	•	recognizable interaction patterns unique to another product  
  
The goal is not imitation.  
The goal is to learn what makes a guided health experience feel complete, trusted, and repeatable—then express that through mediCalm’s own medically grounded identity.
