# MediCalm Product Vision
Version: v1
Status: Draft
Owner: Josh
Depends on: None

## Purpose

This document defines what MediCalm is, who it serves, what makes it different, and the experience standard it must uphold.

## Product Definition

MediCalm is a personalized body-regulation system that helps users reduce pain, tension, and nervous-system overload through adaptive, symptom-driven protocols involving breathing, posture, movement, and guided self-regulation.

MediCalm is not:
- a diagnosis engine
- a replacement for urgent medical care
- a generic meditation or breathing app
- a static exercise library

MediCalm is:
- medically informed
- protocol-driven
- feedback-adaptive
- sequence-aware
- cross-platform
- visually calm and trustworthy

## Core Problem

People often feel tight, burned out, compressed, inflamed, overstimulated, or stuck in painful body states, but they do not know what to do in the moment. Existing tools are often too generic, too vague, too spiritualized, or too medically intimidating.

Users need:
- immediate guidance
- medically reassuring framing
- simple actions that feel safe
- protocols that adapt to symptom patterns
- short sessions that work during real life

## Core Solution

MediCalm converts a user’s current body state into a short guided protocol using:
- structured medical truth
- mechanism-based interpretation
- protocol selection logic
- guided session delivery
- feedback-driven improvement

## Target Experience

The app should feel:
- calm
- competent
- trustworthy
- modern
- minimal
- medically reassuring

The app should never feel:
- alarmist
- flashy
- gimmicky
- hyper-clinical
- preachy
- wellness-generic

## User Outcome

The product goal is not to “fix everything.”

The product goal is to help users:
- feel safer in their body
- reduce tension and symptom intensity
- understand what kind of state they are in
- take guided action
- learn which protocols work best for them over time

## Differentiation

What makes MediCalm different:
- mechanism-driven rather than symptom-only
- sequence-aware rather than single-exercise based
- medically grounded rather than generic wellness-driven
- feedback-adaptive rather than static
- calm and reassuring rather than high-energy or gamified

## Platform Scope

MediCalm should be designed for:
- desktop compatibility
- iPhone compatibility
- responsive layouts
- touch-friendly interfaces
- low cognitive load in active symptom states

## Product Philosophy

Truth → Logic → Execution → Experience

MediCalm should always prioritize:
1. safety
2. clarity
3. calm usability
4. structured guidance
5. personalization over time

## Immediate Product Standard

Before implementation, all product decisions should support this standard:

> MediCalm must look clean, feel reassuring, and guide people through informed, structured calming actions grounded in trusted medical and therapeutic reasoning.
