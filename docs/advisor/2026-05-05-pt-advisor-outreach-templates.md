# PT Advisor Outreach Templates

**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Purpose:** Three outreach templates for sourcing the M7.4 PT advisor — friction-matched per relationship type. Adapt each per recipient with specific knowledge.

**Sender context (for tone calibration, not copy):**
- JSEer — MIS CS Honors, SDSU graduate
- Solo developer (lived-experience motivation)
- mediCalm targets users in moments of distress with breath/regulation pathways
- Documentation-first, PMID-grounded discipline
- Not a credentialed clinician — needs PT advisor for clinical defensibility

**Target advisor profile:**
- PT credential (DPT or equivalent)
- Active or recent clinical practice
- Demonstrated interest in breath / somatic / regulation work (not just ortho)
- Comfort evaluating working software prototypes

## Template 1 — Warm-intro (existing connections)

**Use for:** SDSU Exercise & Nutritional Sciences classmates, PTs in your existing network, clinical health contacts you've worked with directly. Personal frame; references shared context.

**Channel:** Direct message, email, or text — whichever you've used before with this person.

**Subject line (if email):** `Quick question — building a clinical breathwork tool, would you take a look?`

---

> Hey [Name],
>
> Hope you're doing well — [shared-context sentence: "still grinding at [their workplace]?", "miss those [shared-experience] days", or whatever fits].
>
> I'm building a tool I'd genuinely value your read on. It's called mediCalm — a clinical breath and regulation support system for people in moments of musculoskeletal pain, tension, or stress. The idea is structured, PMID-grounded breath sessions with phase-based progression — diaphragmatic onboarding, primary breath work, optional positional holds, integration. Currently in development; no public release.
>
> The reason I'm reaching out specifically: I'm at a milestone where the engineering substrate is built but the clinical content (~120 pre-written sessions across ~12 pathways) needs PT review before lock. I'd love to have someone with your background look at the partition logic — *which clinical signals drive which session variant* — before the library gets authored against it.
>
> Phase 1 is about 3-5 hours, doc-based, foldable into a coffee conversation. Later phases (M7.2-M7.4) are experiential — using working sessions, judging defensibility through clinical UX. Total estimate is 26-40 hours over the project, paid (or equity / advisory credit / combo — open to whatever works for you).
>
> Worth a 30-minute conversation? I'd send over a one-pager with the scope and the work to date so you can see what you'd be looking at. No commitment from the call.
>
> [Your name]

---

- [ ] Adjust opener tone if relationship is more/less casual
- [ ] If the recipient has specific work in breath/regulation/somatics, name it ("your work on [X] is exactly the kind of context that would make this review valuable")

## Sourcing question to think about before sending

mediCalm's advisor surface is different from THE MUSCLE PT's. THE MUSCLE PT's advisor space is movement/ortho clinical (thoracic, scapular, central sensitization). mediCalm's advisor needs breath/regulation/distress expertise — overlapping but distinct.

A vanilla sports-PT without somatic/regulation interest can evaluate movement mechanics but won't catch issues specific to breathwork sequencing in distress. SDSU Exercise & Nutritional Sciences faculty + clinical alumni in regulation-adjacent practice is probably a stronger starting pool than generic LinkedIn search.

**10-minute pre-outreach pass:** identify 1-2 candidate names from your network — even rough leads make personalization meaningful. Sources to consider:
- SDSU School of Exercise & Nutritional Sciences faculty list — anyone teaching breath, somatic, or autonomic regulation content
- Alumni in clinical regulation-adjacent practice (PTs working in pain clinics, performance settings with breath integration, somatic experiencing practitioners with PT credentials)
- Podcasts you've listened to where breath/regulation/PT intersect — host or guest as candidate
- Published papers you've cited in M7.0 — Shaffer & Meehan, Laborde, Meehan & Shaffer themselves are not realistic targets, but their co-authors or successors at adjacent institutions might be reachable

If candidates surface from this pass, personalize Template 1 or 2 to them specifically. If no candidates surface, send Template 3 to 2-3 LinkedIn-discoverable PTs whose published work aligns, and let cold outreach become the lead-generation step.

## Cadence

- **Send 2-3 outreach this week.** Mix templates per relationship type.
- **Follow up after 5-7 business days** if no response. One follow-up; don't push beyond that.
- **Goal: 1-2 introductory conversations within 14 days.** This unblocks M7.2 execution per the parallel-workstream framing.
- **If no responses in 14 days:** broaden the candidate pool (add 2-3 more names) and send another batch. Don't pause M7.2 indefinitely; if sourcing slips beyond 4 weeks, M7.2 execution proceeds without advisor input and Phase 1 lands at M7.4 in the worst case.
