# PT Advisor Engagement — mediCalm

## What mediCalm is
A clinical breath and regulation support tool for people in moments of
musculoskeletal pain, tension, or stress. The product delivers structured,
PMID-grounded breath sessions with phase-based progression — diaphragmatic
onboarding, primary breath work, optional positional holds, integration.
Currently in development; no public release. Solo developer (lived-experience
motivation), documentation-first discipline, no diagnosis claims, safety-first
throughout.

## What we are building right now
A foundation milestone (internally "M7") that introduces a curated catalog
of ~120 multi-phase sessions. Each session is pre-written, pre-reviewed,
and delivered as a fixed catalog — not generated from rules at runtime.
Sessions are organized into ~12 named pathways (e.g., thoracic-restrictive
with anxious overlay, lumbo-pelvic decompression, generalized
down-regulation), with each pathway carrying ~10 variants conditioned on
the user's irritability pattern, flare sensitivity, and baseline pain
intensity band.

## What review is needed

The high-value review is **experiential** — clinical defensibility judged through
using working sessions — not a spreadsheet of citations. mediCalm's Tier A grounding
(physiological claims with verified PMIDs + exact figures) is structurally
pre-validated through a closed-loop research workflow before variants ship; the
advisor's role is to assess clinical UX, sequencing defensibility, and partition
correctness as the product matures, rather than verify citations.

1. **Partition + intent alignment.** Are irritability × flare sensitivity ×
   baseline intensity band the right clinical signals to drive variant choice
   within a pathway, or are there others a working PT would treat as primary?
   Document-based; foldable into the introductory conversation.

2. **Working prototype review.** Once intro / narrated transitions / closing
   land in the product, experience a representative set of variants end-to-end.
   Judge clinical defensibility through use: does the session structure feel
   right, does the cue language read clinically, does the pacing match how a PT
   would sequence equivalent work?

3. **Position holds + mid-session controls review.** Once positional phases and
   pause/skip/resume controls land, repeat experiential review with the richer
   phase set. Particular focus on positional cues, entry/exit instructions, and
   the safety of mid-session control behavior.

4. **Library lock review.** Full library + UX context built up. Final pass on
   clinical defensibility across the catalog, with recommendations for any
   variant that should not ship.

## Estimated time commitment
26–40 hours total across the project lifecycle, distributed as the product
matures. Sliceable into phases:

| Phase | Scope | When | Estimate |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Partition + intent alignment | At M7.0 / M7.1 (doc-based) | ~3–5 hrs |
| 2 | Working prototype review | At M7.2 (experiential) | ~8–12 hrs |
| 3 | Position holds + controls review | At M7.3 (experiential) | ~5–8 hrs |
| 4 | Library lock review | At M7.4 (full library + UX context) | ~10–15 hrs |

Phase 1 is the highest-leverage early input — running it before M7.1
implementation lets us settle the conditioning dims with clinical confirmation
rather than as a working assumption. Phases 2–4 are experiential: the advisor
uses working sessions and judges defensibility through clinical UX, not through
citation review. Tier A grounding is structurally pre-validated through a
closed-loop research workflow that produces verified PMIDs + exact figures
before variants reach review; advisor focus stays on usage-defensibility and
clinical UX rather than citation verification.

## Why this work matters

reviewable, every clinical claim grounded, every variant traceable
to its conditioning logic — is what separates "wellness app with PT
vocabulary" from "clinically defensible session delivery." Your
review is what moves mediCalm from PT-vocabulary positioning to
clinical defensibility.
